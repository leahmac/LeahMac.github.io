In the old days when we spent our summers in Girvan, there was one year a dour milk boy who delivered our milk every day to the front step; he had a face like a torn welly, so he did. He was a big lad, working for his father, no doubt; they’d arrive in the street with cart and horse (aroma of horses and warm milk and sounds of cartwheels on rough road) and that one could never crack a smile. We would tease him then, you know, and comment on what a big smile he had…what gaiety he brought to our front door every day. "Here he is, that laughing boy", Dad would shout to us in the kitchen. Sullen, unwilling to see the joke he grumbled back “u’m no’ laughin’”. Woe betide the sulker, now, who tries to impress a family group with tales of discontent...they'll leave with "u'm no laughin'" and shrieks of laughter ringing in their ears.
