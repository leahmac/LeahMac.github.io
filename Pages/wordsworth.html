Since risen from ocean, ocean to defy
Appeared the crag of Ailsa, ne’er did morn
With gleaming lights more gracefully adorn
His sides, or wreathe with mist his forehead high.

William Wordsworth, 1833

If Ailsa Craig has mist at her hat
It’s a sure sign it’s going to be wet,
But if Ailsa, she has mist at her feet,
You can be sure there’s going to be heat.

I discover a painting of Ailsa Craig and Turnberry Golf Course in the entranceway of the UBC Golf Club, and my chest lurches with memory. I stand frozen on the lurid carpet, with no audience for my story.
