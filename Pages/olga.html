Someone always asks, how did I become Olga? which is the name I have always been called. It was fashionable, and so essential, to call the first granddaughter after the maternal Grandmother, so I had that honour, but there was already in the house my Aunt Jean of the same name, as well as my Gran.

In 1919 the Russians were being brought to everyone’s attention because of the Revolution, and so ‘Olga’ was a name more often heard, and in order to use the O of my middle name (Ogilvie) it was decided that I would be Olga.
